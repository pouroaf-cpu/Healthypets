## 1b. Expanded hub architecture (cats + dogs)

Four content territories, tiered by how well they actually monetise:

| Territory | NZ vol/mo (sampled) | Affiliate strength | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Parasite control** — flea, flea+worm combos, worming, tick | ~53,000 | strong (KD 0, recurring) | **Money core** |
| **2. Supplements & health products** — joint, gut/probiotic, skin/coat, dental treats, calming | ~10,000+ | good (product, recurring) | **Money** |
| **3. Food** — best cat/dog food, life-stage, grain-free | ~5,500 | weak (low margin, brand-loyal) | Traffic + light money |
| **4. Pet health Q&A** — "can I give my pet X", safety, symptoms | ~3,500+ | none (informational) | Traffic feeder → links into 1 & 2 |

**Cautions (be honest about these):**
- **Food** is a weak affiliate earner (low commission on low-margin food); the "comparison"
  search itself is tiny — volume sits in "best [cat/dog] food nz" and "grain free". Grain-free
  is a controversial nutrition topic (heart-disease debate) → YMYL-adjacent, vet-cited only.
  Use food for traffic + cross-sell, not as the monetisation backbone.
- **Medication / "can I give my pet X"** is high-traffic but **YMYL + liability**: answers are
  usually "no / see a vet", commercial intent is low, and dosing advice is dangerous. Use as
  top-of-funnel trust content (honest answer, vet citations, NO human-med dosing, disclaimers)
  that internally links to the money pages and captures email — never a money page itself.

**Sequence:** lead with Territory 1 (cat flea pillar) → expand into 2 → use 3 & 4 as feeders.

## 2. The model — affiliate content hub, NOT a retailer

We are building a **content + affiliate authority site**, not an e-commerce store.

| | Affiliate hub (this plan) | E-commerce store (rejected) |
|---|---|---|
| Build | Reviews, comparisons, how-to guides | Inventory/dropship, fulfilment, support |
| Capital | Low (domain + hosting + content) | High (stock, ops, returns, CX) |
| Competes with | Partners *with* Pet Direct/Petstock | Fights Animates/Petstock head-on |
| Risk | Low, content-led | High, operational |

**Rationale:** the research validated *demand* and an *affiliate money path* — it did
**not** justify entering NZ's saturated pet-retail market. Build the audience first; a
white-label/dropship store can be bolted on later *on top of* traffic you already own.

## 3. How affiliate companies actually work

Two structures, plus Amazon as its own thing:

1. **Affiliate networks** (Commission Factory, Awin, Linkshop, Rakuten, Impact)
   A middleman platform hosting many merchants' programs. You join the network once,
   then apply to individual merchant programs inside it. The network handles tracking,
   reporting, and consolidated payment.
2. **Direct / in-house programs** — some retailers run their own; you apply on their site.
3. **Amazon Associates** — Amazon's own program. NZ residents can join, but there's no
   Amazon NZ store, so links go to amazon.com.au/.com (lower relevance for NZ shoppers).

**The mechanics, step by step:**
1. **Apply** to the network/program (they usually require a live website to approve you).
2. On approval you get a **unique tracking link / ID** per merchant.
3. You place those links in your content ("Buy at Pet Direct →").
4. A reader clicks → a **cookie** is set (typical window **7–30 days**).
5. If they buy *anything* within that window, the sale is **attributed to you**.
6. You earn a **commission** — pet category runs **~3–7%** of the sale.
7. The network tracks confirmed sales and **pays out monthly** once you clear a
   **threshold** (often NZ$50–100), via bank transfer or PayPal.

**What matters for earnings:** cookie window length, commission %, average order value,
and your conversion rate. Recurring products help because the same reader buys again.

## 7. How the money flows

```
Reader searches "best cat flea treatment nz"
   → lands on your pillar page
   → reads comparison, clicks "Check price at Pet Direct →"  (cookie set, 30 days)
   → buys $45 flea treatment (+ maybe more in the cart)
   → you earn ~5% commission = ~$2.25+, paid monthly by Linkshop
```

## 7a. Capturing the REPEAT purchase (the cookie-window problem)

**Critical:** a single click only credits you for ~30 days. When the customer re-treats
in 8 weeks, that repeat sale is **NOT** automatically yours — they must click your link
*again*, or be on a tracked subscription. The recurrence pays off only if we engineer it:

1. **Repeat clicks via SEO + email (primary).** They re-search → your page still ranks →
   they click again (fresh cookie). To not rely on luck, **capture email from day one**
   (lead magnet: "free flea & worming reminder calendar") and email them *exactly when
   the re-treat is due* with the affiliate link. We manufacture the repeat click.
2. **Subscription / auto-ship products (passive recurring).** Some programs pay recurring
   commission when a referred customer subscribes to auto-delivery — one signup pays every
   cycle. **Deliberately steer readers to "subscribe & save" options.**
   → ACTION: confirm with Pet Direct/Petstock affiliate managers whether they pay on
     recurring auto-ship orders. This materially changes the economics.
3. **Longer cookie windows.** Prefer programs offering 60–90 day cookies — covers more of
   the re-treat cycle per click.
4. **New-customer bounty / lifetime attribution.** Ask each program (rare in retail).

> Implication: **email capture is non-negotiable**, and subscription products are the
> priority to promote — they're the only true "click once, earn repeatedly" path.

## 9. KPIs

- **Leading:** articles published, pages indexed, impressions/clicks (GSC), affiliate-link CTR.
- **Lagging:** organic sessions, affiliate clicks, EPC (earnings per click), commission/month.
- **6-month target:** category pages ranking page 1–2 for long-tail; first recurring
  commission; data on which categories convert best.

## 10. Tech stack (lean)

- **Domain:** something brandable + NZ-signalling (e.g. *.co.nz or a brand name).
- **CMS:** WordPress (fastest for content + affiliate plugins) or Astro (faster site, more dev).
- **Plugins/tools:** affiliate link manager (cloaking + tracking), comparison-table block,
  Google Search Console + GA4, schema for product/review.
- **Disclosure:** clear affiliate disclosure on every page (FTC/Commerce Commission good practice).

## 11. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| YMYL-adjacent (pet *health*) | Stay product/comparison-focused; cite vets; avoid medical claims; add author/vet review. |
| Prescription products vet-only | Focus on OTC flea/worming/supplements that retailers actually sell + pay commission on. |
| Thin single-niche volume | The hub bundles 9 categories (~65k/mo) — already addressed. |
| Affiliate approval needs live site | Build Phase-1 content first, then apply. |
| Seasonality (flea peaks summer) | Cross-category mix + worming/joint/gut smooth the year. |
