# Audience Persona & Content Guardrails

> **This is the brief every article — and every AI writing one — must stick to.**
> Write for one person: the persona below. If a draft wouldn't help *her*, it's wrong.

## NZ market context (real data, 2024)

- **63%** of NZ households own a pet. **Cats are #1** (~1.26M, ~40% of homes); **dogs ~830k** (~31%).
- Cat ownership peaks at **45–64**; **households with children over-index** (66–71% vs 60% no kids).
- **64% of owners spend NZ$1,000–3,000/year per pet** (excl. vet). Dogs ~$1,430–4,250/yr.
- 85–96% desex, 72–89% microchip → owners are **responsible and engaged**, not casual.

## Primary persona — "Megan, 41" (Tauranga)

**The researching, caring, value-conscious pet parent. ~70% of our traffic should be her.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Age / who** | 38–52, female (pet-care purchasing skews strongly female), in a relationship, 1–2 school-aged kids |
| **Where** | Suburban NZ — Tauranga / Hamilton / Christchurch (not central Auckland), owns or paying off a home |
| **Work** | Part-time or full-time — admin, healthcare, teaching, retail management. Busy. |
| **Household income** | ~NZ$85,000–120,000 household. Comfortable but watches spending; not wealthy. |
| **Pets** | A cat (often two) **and/or** a dog — treated as family members ("fur babies"). Names them, posts photos. |
| **Spend** | NZ$1,000–3,000/yr per pet. Will pay for quality if convinced it's worth it; resents being ripped off. |

**What she looks like / her life:** drives an SUV or hatchback, school runs, Kmart + Pak'nSave + the
occasional Mecca treat. Phone-first (browses on iPhone at night on the couch). Member of 2–3 NZ
Facebook pet groups. Watches the odd YouTube how-to. Trusts her vet but finds them expensive and
doesn't want to book a $90 consult for a flea question.

**What she likes / values:** doing right by her pet; practical advice that respects her time and
budget; honesty over hype; "natural where sensible" but pragmatic (she'll use a chemical flea
treatment that actually works). Loves a genuine recommendation and a good deal.

## What she searches (mapped to our data)

She moves through stages — our content must meet each:

1. **Problem / symptom:** "why is my cat scratching", "dog diarrhea treatment", "fleas on cats"
2. **Research / how-to:** "how to get rid of fleas on cats", "how often to worm a dog", "treating fleas on cats"
3. **Comparison (high intent):** "best cat flea treatment nz", "Advantage vs Revolution", "best dog food nz"
4. **Value check:** "cheapest flea treatment for cats nz", "is X worth it"
5. **Safety / reassurance:** "can I give my dog…", "is X safe for kittens"

She also searches: brand names (Black Hawk, Royal Canin, Purina, Advantage, Bravecto), retailers
(Animates, Pet Direct), and life-stage terms (kitten, puppy, senior).

## Her pain points & objections (answer these on every page)

- "Is this **safe** for my pet?" → vet-informed reassurance, cite sources, flag age/weight cautions.
- "Which one is **best for MY pet**?" → comparison table, clear recommendation, by age/type.
- "Is it **worth the money**?" → honest value framing; name budget *and* premium picks.
- "Can I get it **cheaper / in NZ**?" → NZ retailers, NZ$ pricing, current options.
- "Am I doing this **right**?" → schedules, step-by-steps, what-not-to-do.

## What earns her trust (and the click)

- NZ-specific: NZ products, NZ retailers, NZ$, NZ seasons (flea season = summer).
- A real recommendation, not a wall of options. She wants to be *told* the best pick.
- Plain English, warm, knowledgeable-friend tone — never preachy or salesy.
- Honesty (incl. "the cheap one is fine here" / "skip this one") — builds the trust that converts.

## Secondary segments (write for Megan first, but don't exclude these)
- **New pet parent (25–35):** just got a kitten/puppy — anxious, googling everything. High Q&A traffic.
- **Older cat owner (55–70):** devoted, less price-sensitive, senior-pet health (joints, kidney, dental).

## Content rules for AI writers (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Always**
1. Open by **answering her question** in the first 2–3 sentences (helpful before salesy).
2. Be **NZ-specific**: NZ retailers (Pet Direct, Petstock, Animates), NZ$, NZ availability/seasons.
3. Give a **clear recommendation** + a comparison table; name a **budget** and a **premium** pick.
4. **Vet-informed** for any health claim; cite reputable sources; note age/weight/breed cautions.
5. Include the **affiliate disclosure** and an **email-capture** hook (re-treat reminder lead magnet).
6. Cross-link: spokes → pillar, pillar → sibling pillars; suggest the **complete protocol** (basket).
7. Reading level ~Year 8–9. Warm, plain, NZ voice. Short paragraphs, scannable headings.

**Never**
1. Scaremonger or guilt-trip the reader.
2. Give **dosing for human medications** or prescribe — for meds, say "talk to your vet".
3. Make medical/curative claims you can't support; this is **YMYL-adjacent** — accuracy is safety.
4. Publish generic global content (US brands/prices) — it must read as written for NZ.
5. Pad with thin filler; every section must earn its place for Megan.

> Litmus test before publishing: *"Would Megan finish this, trust it, and click 'Check price at
> Pet Direct'?"* If not, fix it.
