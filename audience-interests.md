# Audience — What They Search & What Else They're Into

> Companion to `audience-persona.md`. This is the behavioural/interest map (not a
> persona) — use it for content ideas, social channels, partnerships, and where to
> expand later. Pet-search items are from our DataForSEO research; broader-interest
> items are informed inference from NZ demographics (flagged as such).

## What they search — around pets (data-backed)

Beyond our core health territories, NZ pet owners actively search:

- **Breeds & getting a pet:** dog/cat breeds (Maine Coon, golden retriever, etc.),
  "kittens/puppies for sale", SPCA, breeders, adoption. *(High volume in competitor data.)*
- **Food & nutrition:** brand comparisons (Black Hawk, Royal Canin, Purina, Whiskas),
  best food, puppy/kitten/senior food, grain-free, raw.
- **Parasite & health (our core):** flea, worming, joint, gut, skin, dental, allergies, ears.
- **Accessories:** beds, kennels, toys, collars/leads, litter, bowls, crates.
- **Behaviour & training:** puppy/toilet training, barking, scratching, anxiety.
- **Local services:** groomers, boarding/catteries, "vet near me", dog walkers, daycare.
- **Money & safety:** "cheapest …", pet insurance, "can I give my dog … ", symptoms/"why is my cat …".

**Implication:** the hub's natural expansion order is health → food → behaviour/training → breeds/getting-a-pet.
Each is a future territory; health monetises best, so it leads.

## What else they're into — broader life (inference from NZ demographics)

"Megan" (woman 38–52, partner + kids, suburban NZ homeowner, ~$85–120k household). Likely affinities:

- **Home & garden** — suburban homeowner: gardening, lawn care, DIY, Mitre 10 / Bunnings.
- **Family & parenting** — school-aged kids: school stuff, kids' activities, family meals.
- **Value & deals** — cost-of-living conscious: Pak'nSave, Kmart, Briscoes sales, **Trade Me** bargains, "cheapest" everything.
- **Food** — cooking & baking for the family; meal planning.
- **Her own wellness** — fitness, supplements, self-care (mirrors how she thinks about her pet's health).
- **NZ lifestyle** — camping, the beach, domestic road trips, the bach.
- **Media** — Facebook (in 2–3 pet groups), Instagram, parenting/true-crime podcasts, occasional YouTube how-to.

> These are informed inferences for tone, channel choice and partnerships — not measured data.
> Validate later via Facebook Audience Insights or a reader survey.

## So what (actionable)

- **Tone & framing:** speak like a practical, money-aware NZ parent — value + care, never preachy.
- **Channels:** NZ pet **Facebook groups** + **Instagram** are where she is; that's the distribution play.
- **Partnership/affiliate adjacencies:** pet insurance, NZ pet retailers, possibly garden/home brands later.
- **Content hooks that resonate:** "best value", "is it worth it", "safe for kittens/puppies",
  seasonal (flea season = summer), "what the vet won't tell you for free".
- **Future expansion:** food → training → breeds; and the *audience* (suburban NZ mums) opens
  adjacent niches (garden, home, family budgeting) if you ever want to widen beyond pets.
